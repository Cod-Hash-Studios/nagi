# Nagi brand

`凪` is the quiet moment when the wind drops and the sea becomes still.

That is the promise behind Nagi: agents can keep moving while the surface stays
calm enough for a human to understand what matters.

## The feeling

Nagi should feel light, composed, and tactile. It is a modern Japanese editorial
brand, not a cyberpunk terminal brand. Think warm paper, ink, open water, one
clear vermilion signal, and plenty of room to breathe.

The product can be technically deep. The brand should never make that depth feel
heavy.

## Palette

| Color | Hex | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| Rice paper | `#F7F1E3` | Primary canvas |
| Deep indigo | `#23395B` | Type, structure, primary ink |
| Seafoam | `#80CFC4` | Motion, secondary states, calm water |
| Vermilion | `#EF624D` | Attention, the sun, decisive actions |
| Slate | `#526575` | Supporting copy |

Vermilion is a signal, not a background color. Use it once and let it matter.

## Voice

- Calm, not passive.
- Clear, not simplified to the point of vagueness.
- Confident, not grandiose.
- Human, not stuffed with AI jargon.
- Honest about what works today and what is still under construction.

Prefer: “One mission. A calm surface.”

Avoid: “The revolutionary AI-native orchestration platform of the future.”

## Assets

- `assets/brand/nagi-mark.svg`: compact product mark.
- `assets/brand/nagi-lockup.svg`: primary horizontal wordmark.
- `assets/brand/nagi-hero.png`: generated editorial hero artwork.
- `assets/brand/nagi-flow.svg`: animated mission story for GitHub and docs.

Keep clear space around the mark equal to the radius of the vermilion sun. Do
not recolor individual waves, add glow effects, or place the lockup over a busy
dark background.

## Image direction

Use contemporary Japanese collage, washi texture, restrained sumi-e brushwork,
still coastal landscapes, indigo water, and small vermilion accents. Favor
daylight and negative space.

Avoid neon grids, glowing terminals, circuit boards, robots, floating code,
server racks, and generic “AI brain” imagery.
