# Code of conduct

Nagi should be a place where people can disagree precisely without making each
other smaller.

## Expected behavior

- Be direct about code and ideas, respectful toward people.
- Give reproducible evidence and acknowledge uncertainty.
- Make room for contributors with different experience, language, identity, and
  communication styles.
- Respect privacy. Remove prompts, tokens, private source, and personal data from
  reports and examples.
- Accept a maintainer's request to pause, move, or narrow a heated discussion.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, deliberate
misgendering, doxxing, sustained personal attacks, spam, and publishing private
security details are not accepted in project spaces or project-related private
contact.

## Reporting and enforcement

For conduct concerns, contact the maintainers privately through the repository
owner's GitHub contact channel. For vulnerabilities, use the private process in
`SECURITY.md`. Do not ask a person involved in the incident to adjudicate it.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, pause a thread, issue a private warning,
temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban an account. Responses
should be proportionate, documented privately, and avoid exposing the reporter.
A maintainer who is the subject of a report must recuse themselves.

This policy applies to the repository, issue tracker, discussions, reviews,
community spaces, and private communication undertaken in a project role.
